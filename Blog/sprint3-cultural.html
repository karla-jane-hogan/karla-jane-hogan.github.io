Emotional Intelligence

Emotional Intelligence is a collection of skills - the ability to be aware of your own feelings - identifying your emotions, how you think and act on those emotions. How you relate to others and social communication. There are three steps of Emotional Intelligence including;

  • Attention training (Mindfulness)
  • Self knowledge and Mastery
  • Creating useful and mental habits

    • How is it different to IQ?

    EI is about the emotions and how you feel, thinking about those emotions and how you act consciously with those emotions. IQ is based on visual processing, working memory, fluid & quantitative reasoning.

    Why is it important?

    It is important to learn the EI skills to be the best version of yourself and to operate in an optimal state. Your mind, body and soul to be in alignment, which can cultivate a clear, calm mind to deal with everyday life. To be aware of your emotions and how acting consciously through them and using them as a tool to relate to others is very helpful in difficult situations. In the workplace the three benefits that are achieved through learning EI skills are; Stellar performance, Outstanding Leadership, and Happiness. This helps and benefits yourself and everyone in the workplace environment.
